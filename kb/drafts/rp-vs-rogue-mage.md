## Opener

Keep a solid distance between yourself and your priest, and once your priest gets sapped keep staying semi-far until the enemy mage spellsteals him a few times.

If the enemy team is horde and you are playing a Human rogue, this is where you would be popping your perception and scouting around the priest and the mage for the enemy rogue.

I like to spam [[ability:shadowstep]] > [[ability:cheap-shot]] on the rogue at this point rather than [[ability:sap]], because if I [[ability:sap]] him it gives the enemy mage time to fish me out of stealth, and if I [[ability:cheap-shot]] it gives them less time to react and adapt.

Assuming you land the [[ability:cheap-shot]] on the rogue, you [[ability:premed]] > [[ability:cheap-shot]] > [[ability:hemo]] once > [[ability:kidney-shot]], and if he sits the kidney you [[ability:vanish]] [[ability:garrote]] > [[ability:rupture]] towards the end of your kidney and quickly [[ability:preparation]] and [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]] [[ability:vanish]] to cover yourself from mage AoE, then finish the rogue off with a half [[ability:cheap-shot]] into a [[ability:rupture]].

## If enemy opens first

### When You Don't Get the Opener

Most of the time, especially vs an alliance rogue mage where the enemy rogue is human, you should assume he has goggles. He is much more likely to find you than you are to find him.

This is why typically you just give up the opener and let them [[ability:sap]] your priest. As the enemy mage spellsteals your priest and looks to shatter him, you [[ability:premed]] > [[ability:deadly-throw]] the mage from max range from stealth and then immediately [[ability:vanish]] away, giving your priest time to shield himself and get around the corner.

At this stage the enemy mage has to run away and play for himself, as your priest can slowly start 1v1ing him.

However, most of the time the enemy rogue ends up opening on your priest along with the mage, even despite you stopping his shatter, in which case you try to [[ability:shadowstep]] [[ability:cheap-shot]] the enemy rogue immediately and put him into a [[ability:kidney-shot]]. If he trinkets you [[ability:blind]] him and [[ability:vanish]] away, typically with a [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]] as well to cover yourself from mage AoE.

Then [[ability:sap]] the rogue off of the [[ability:blind]] to stall stun DR's and [[ability:cheap-shot]] [[ability:kidney-shot]] him again — he should be dead at this point.

You may also want to hold onto your last [[ability:vanish]] and let your priest [[ability:fear]] the rogue out of [[ability:blind]], or even [[ability:fear]] him before you [[ability:blind]]. Whatever is most convenient in the moment.

### If They Open on You

A lot of the time the rogue mage might find you, in which case your priest should immediately [[ability:pain-suppression]] you if the rogue mage's intention is to kill you. That way you can save your trinket and potentially [[ability:vanish]] [[ability:cheap-shot]] [[ability:kidney-shot]] the enemy rogue as you come out of the stunlock.

In the case that the enemy rogue vanishes away at this point, you have to fully commit to the mage. Typically just blow [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]] and run him down because you're still on stun DR so you need to make the most out of it.

If your priest is unable to give you [[ability:pain-suppression]] because he is imp CS'd, then trinket the [[ability:kidney-shot]] as quickly as you can and [[ability:vanish]] > [[ability:premed]] > [[ability:cheap-shot]] the enemy rogue and [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]] to cover yourself from sheeps and just try to kill the rogue. If he trinkets the [[ability:kidney-shot]] and blinds you before you get a chance to blind him — in the event that the rogue vanishes after you trinket kidney, you need to [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]] and run down the mage. Use sprint/[[ability:shadowstep]] and even [[ability:preparation]] > [[ability:shadowstep]] if you have to. You need to pressure them as quickly as you can while on stun DR.

If your priest is sheeped however and there's no imp CS available for the enemy team, your priest should trinket the first sheep and [[ability:pain-suppression]] you the moment they open on you. Your trinket is generally more valuable than your priest's trinket in this matchup.

Priest should always be dotting the enemy rogue too if your [[ability:blind]] has already been used, as it forces the rogue to [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]] it to get a clean [[ability:vanish]] off.

The general gist is to stop the enemy mage's shatter combo at the start with deadly throw from stealth into a vanish and play from there. Whenever the rogue is out of stealth, your priority is to stun him and blind/[[ability:fear]] when he trinkets. Do not get sapped under any circumstance.
