## Opener

You have a few options depending on whether you're facing an Alliance or Horde team.

If the enemy team is Alliance you can abandon your priest and hide somewhere far away. The moment the enemy priest runs in you pop your perception (if Human) and [[ability:sap]] the enemy priest and look for the enemy rogue.

If you've sapped the priest and you're playing Human, spam [[ability:shadowstep]] [[ability:cheap-shot]] on the rogue rather than [[ability:sap]], since the enemy priest is already in a [[ability:sap]]. However, if the [[ability:sap]] is half duration left, spam [[ability:shadowstep]] [[ability:sap]] on the enemy rogue instead.

If you are not Human and you are facing a Human rogue, abandon your priest entirely and find a safe hiding spot. Once the enemy team rushes in towards your priest you can maybe sneak a [[ability:sap]] on the enemy priest by sprinting behind him, but only if you feel certain the enemy rogue is not nearby.

You can also just let them [[ability:sap]] your priest, dispel him, and sprint in late and [[ability:shadowstep]] [[ability:cheap-shot]] the enemy rogue and [[ability:will-of-the-forsaken]] out of the first [[ability:fear]] and put him into a [[ability:gouge]] into a [[ability:kidney-shot]].

## If enemy opens first

### If They Get the Opener

If the enemy team gets the initial stun on you, [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]] out of [[ability:kidney-shot]] typically, depending on your HP and wound stacks. If healthy enough, hold your [[ability:vanish]] and start shiving crippling into wounds on the enemy rogue, building up for a 5cp [[ability:shadowstep]] [[ability:kidney-shot]].

## Mid-fight rotation

### Mid-Game

I like to just sit on the enemy rogue, unless the opener granted a big opportunity to keep going priest.

Stun/[[ability:gouge]] the enemy rogue on cooldown and track crippling/wound poison on both yourself and the enemy rogue. Any time you stun the rogue and dump all your energy into him, immediately run behind a pillar after and line of sight him, pooling energy whilst also stalling stun DR's. If he [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]] to catch up, press [[ability:evasion]] immediately and put your back against a wall.

If he ever cloaks to catch up or get rid of wounds, [[ability:blind]] him and shiv the enemy priest and look to restealth. While restealthing, unless you have enough time to comfortably [[ability:sap]] the enemy rogue, tell your priest to keep the enemy rogue in combat.

Rotate your stuns, play efficiently with your cooldowns, and oom the enemy priest by having better uptime on the enemy rogue. Keep your wounds up.

If you're running past the priest and don't already have valuable combo points on the rogue, shiving the priest with crippling poison is big value. You can really abuse the fact that there's no poison dispel in this matchup.

Save your [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]] for when you have a lot of wounds on you and your priest is able to cast a heal.

Holding [[ability:blind]] for the enemy rogue is something I like to do most of the time. But if the enemy priest uses trinket on a [[ability:fear]] or stun, you always have the option to [[ability:blind]] the enemy priest and bleed the enemy rogue and [[ability:vanish]] > [[ability:sap]] the priest off the blind. Just ensure you've built some pressure on the rogue first.

Play around wound poisons on both you and the enemy rogue. Don't hit into shields if your priest can dispel them. Hold energy and wait for dispel. Play efficiently and play around enemy priest's mana.

## Notes

### If You [[ability:sap]] Priest but Don't Find Rogue

Have your priest dispel the enemy priest and open on him with a [[ability:cheap-shot]] and then immediately shiv crippling poison expecting the enemy rogue to stun you.

At this stage your priest should be getting the first [[ability:fear]] off, preferably on both enemy players. This gives you plenty of opportunity to set up a kill — maybe with a [[ability:sap]] on the enemy rogue out of [[ability:fear]], or simply giving you the option to put up [[ability:evasion]] before the enemy rogue lands his [[ability:kidney-shot]].

If you're getting a decent opener on the enemy priest while maintaining crippling poison on him and putting him into an early [[ability:fear]], there is no reason to go off the priest at this point. Send an early [[ability:blind]] on the enemy rogue if you have developed decent pressure.
