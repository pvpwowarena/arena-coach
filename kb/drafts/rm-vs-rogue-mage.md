## Opener

This matchup is very opener dependent and also mage vs mage dependent. Whoever is winning the 1v1 has the advantage overall, as it will force the enemy rogue to make a play sooner or later.

Crippling should be equipped in off hand at all times in this matchup, and if you feel like you have time, applying mind numbing to main hand when you recognize that you're vs a rogue mage can also be very good, as we don't need the wound poison here.

I'm usually spamming [[ability:step]]> [[ability:premed]]> [[ability:cheap-shot]] on enemy rogue in this matchup. The reason for this is because if the enemy rogue opens on my mage with a garrote, I might accidentally shadowstep to the rogue spamming [[ability:sap]] when he's in combat, and also if I were to [[ability:sap]] the rogue, that gives the enemy mage a chance to knock me out of stealth. Another reason is that it gives the enemy rogue more time to react to the opener on him. And sapping rogue does not always mean that you can safely kill the mage, because the enemy rogue could always trinket the [[ability:sap]] and blind me and vanish himself.

If I ever find the rogue, I press [[ability:shadowstep]]> [[ability:premed]]> [[ability:cheap-shot]]> [[ability:rupture]]> [[ability:hemo]]> 2-3 cp [[ability:kidney]], and then I vanish. At this point the enemy rogue is almost dead usually, assuming that my mage got to connect with some instant damage. If he trinkets the cheap shot, he'll most likely do it too late and still be ruptured, in which case I can trinket anything I want and [[ability:vanish]]> [[ability:cheap-shot]]> [[ability:kidney]] him. If he trinkets early, you have the option to shadowstep kidney him or blind him.

Another option if your mage is struggling in the 1v1, even sometimes if he's not struggling, just to put pressure on the enemy mage, is to invest a [[ability:shadowstep]]> [[ability:garrote]] (or [[ability:ambush]], if you're playing ambush spec) and then vanish immediately after.

The general gist is to spam shadowstep > [[ability:premed]] > cheap shot on the enemy rogue and immediately rupturing after and doing a short kidney into a vanish. You can throw a cloak as well just to make sure you can safely vanish. It's a fast paced matchup that comes down mostly to opener and whichever mage is winning the 1v1.
