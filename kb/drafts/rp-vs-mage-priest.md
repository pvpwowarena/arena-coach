## Opener

I usually spend a sprint to get there as fast as possible before the enemy mage pushes in and gets combat from my priest. It depends on the map however. On Blade's Edge specifically I usually just stay back and have my priest jump down below the bridge and wait for them to come to us, unless they're staying back or slow to move in, in which case I can sprint behind and sneak a [[ability:sap]].

Ideally you want to [[ability:sap]] the priest and have your priest mount in and dispel him and open on him. Out of the [[ability:premed]] > [[ability:cheap-shot]] > [[ability:kidney-shot]] you can put him into a [[ability:fear]] and finish him off with a [[ability:garrote]] usually if you have the most ideal opener. However, this opener is rare and shouldn't ever occur unless the enemy team really messes up.

Usually if you [[ability:sap]] the enemy priest, the enemy mage will gatekeep your priest from reaching him. In which case you dispel the enemy mage and open on him. I like to just [[ability:premed]] > [[ability:cheap-shot]] him, because if I were to [[ability:garrote]] first global I might just get stuck in a frostbite.

## Alternative opener

### Opening on the Mage

Once I've opened with [[ability:cheap-shot]], I wait a second to see what he chooses to do. If he sits the [[ability:cheap-shot]] and doesn't blink, I'll shiv a crippling poison and [[ability:expose-armor]] and hold my energy until my priest has dispelled his power word shield and ice barrier. Then mash [[ability:hemo]] and dump all my energy into him and try to [[ability:blind]] the priest early if he's in range.

If he blinks the [[ability:cheap-shot]], which they usually do, I'll [[ability:shadowstep]] [[ability:kidney-shot]] immediately and cripple shiv his trinket to keep him in place.

Once I've crippled him, unless I'm expecting an Escape Artist racial (if he's a gnome), I'll swap to my mind numbing off hand and apply mind numbing poison. If expecting Escape Artist on the crippling, stick to crippling off hand and immediately re-cripple his Escape Artist.

Mind numbing poison makes the mage's life hell. It's impossible for him to sneak sheeps and it's rather pointless for him to cast at all.

Once I've landed mind numbing + crippling on him, just mash [[ability:hemo]] and nuke him.

Try to [[ability:blind]] the enemy priest early whenever you have created some pressure. Use [[ability:blind]] on the priest most of the time, as the mage can just [[ability:ice-block]] it. The only time to [[ability:blind]] the mage is if he has no trinket, no [[ability:ice-block]], or if he's on hypothermia and you're about to die from his uptime.

## Mid-fight rotation

### Pet Goes & Pillar Play

If the mage pops pet, you can swap to it immediately and kill it in a few hemos, and your priest can pre-dispel the power word shield on the pet.

However, most of the time when the enemy mage pops pet you don't have time to kill it. Typically pop [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]] and shiv another crippling on the mage and run behind a pillar along with your priest. Ideally shiv the enemy priest during your [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]] too if he's nearby.

When you're behind the pillar, focus on good movement and dodge the enemy mage's blizzards by looking at his cast bar and pre-emptively moving side to side. Sometimes you'll even be able to restealth.

If unable to restealth and the enemy mage sends the pet behind the pillar, kill it.

If you manage to restealth and the enemy priest is in the vicinity, open on him with a [[ability:cheap-shot]] > [[ability:kidney-shot]] and potentially [[ability:fear]]/[[ability:blind]] him out of it.

## Notes

### Additional Notes

Blinding stoneform before he manages to use desperate prayer is great. It forces him to sit the [[ability:blind]] and allow your stuns/[[ability:fear]] to come off DR and re-stack wounds/crippling, or trinket and desperate prayer without wound poisons.

Don't overcommit too hard on the mage and don't let him drag you too far away from a pillar, unless you have [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]].

Stop [[ability:mana-burn]], but don't put yourself in a bad position just to stop one. Tanking a burn here and there won't necessarily make you lose.

Shiv crippling poison on both of them whenever convenient. Use [[ability:evasion]] on Shadowfiend as there's nothing else to evasion here.

Enemy [[ability:fear]] are the main concern in this matchup. Shiv the enemy priest whenever convenient and gouge him when he pushes in for a [[ability:fear]]. Even though you spend most time on the mage, the priest is usually the kill target if you catch him in a stunlock without shield.
