## Opener

Option 1 — Kill Druid

If you are human or if the enemy druid is in tree form, you have the option to [[ability:sap]] the warlock and kill the druid. This is my preferred way as it feels the most consistent.

If the druid is in stealth and you find him, open with [[ability:cheap-shot]] > [[ability:hemo]] > [[ability:gouge]] > [[ability:kidney-shot]] and [[ability:blind]] him after the kidney. Depending on how many wounds you procced during the [[ability:cheap-shot]] > [[ability:hemo]] > [[ability:gouge]], either shiv wounds during the kidney or spam [[ability:hemo]] during kidney if you already have enough wounds. The goal is 4-5 wounds as he comes out of kidney.

Once he comes out of kidney, [[ability:blind]] him before he gets any globals off while your priest gets to a pillar near the druid where he can LoS fear. If your priest has already gotten feared at this point he should immediately trinket the very first one.

Your priest can attempt to dot and [[ability:fear]] the druid as he trinkets the [[ability:blind]]. The priest should be dotting the druid BEFORE he [[ability:fear]] to cover devour.

Once the druid trinkets [[ability:blind]] or even if he sits it, you [[ability:vanish]] > [[ability:premed]] > [[ability:garrote]] > [[ability:expose-armor]] once you have 5 combo points and run him down. Have your priest immediately dispel fear off of you.

Press [[ability:preparation]] early to ensure you can [[ability:vanish]] the incoming [[ability:death-coil]] or [[ability:vanish]] a fear cast. Try to save [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]] for nature's grasp, but you can also cloak an incoming fear or [[ability:death-coil]].

This is an all-in and your priest should be popping Shadowfiend and dotting the druid after [[ability:blind]] and just mashing dispel with Power Infusion. He should immediately dispel any fear/coil/nature's grasp off of you.

Double wound at all times while tunneling druid, unless you already have 5 wounds in which case swap to crippling off hand for lucky crippling procs.

Trinket should primarily be saved for a potential NS [[ability:cyclone]], but if the druid is low enough and you have [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]], you can trinket a fear or [[ability:death-coil]] and try to pre-cloak the NS [[ability:cyclone]].

Make sure to press [[ability:evasion]] once the druid goes bear form and don't give him your back for bash. Always face him.

Option 2 — Kill Warlock

Open on the warlock with [[ability:cheap-shot]] > [[ability:expose-armor]] (you only need a 2 combo point [[ability:expose-armor]] with Improved Expose Armor talent to reduce his armor to 0 in season 1, but this changes as warlocks get more armor).

Shiv wounds on the warlock and rotate [[ability:slice-and-dice]]/[[ability:expose-armor]] while shiving crippling and keeping wounds up. Your priest dispels the warlock of any hots and abolish poison.

Whenever the druid is close enough for a [[ability:blind]], press sprint and [[ability:blind]] him. Your priest can potentially [[ability:fear]] the druid too. Make sure to cover the [[ability:fear]] with a rank 1 dot to give a chance of devour dispelling the dot instead of the [[ability:fear]].

Kick fear and keep wounds/crippling up on the warlock and treat him like a PvE encounter. Use [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]] offensively if about to get cycloned or rooted. Only use [[ability:kidney-shot]] on the warlock to stop fears when kick is on cooldown or if about to get rooted.

You have the option to [[ability:vanish]] swap onto the druid, preferably after he trinkets your [[ability:blind]]. Make sure energy is close to 100%, equip double wound, and try to have your priest [[ability:fear]] him as he comes out of [[ability:kidney-shot]] with a dot to prevent devour on the [[ability:fear]].

I suggest killing druid in this matchup. Going warlock is quite complex and requires perfect weapon swap/expose armor/slice and dice. Even then you rely on good poison RNG and bad abolish RNG. When going warlock, you're looking to win on either damage or ooming the enemy druid.
