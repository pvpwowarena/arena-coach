## Opener

### Strategy

Whether you are human or not, assuming you are facing a double human rogue team, stack on top of each other and walk a few yards away from whatever wall or pillar you're on.

When the double rogue team opens on you, typically by sapping both of you and then opening on you (the rogue), your priest has the ability to trinket [[ability:sap]] and [[ability:fear]] the rogues.

If they pre-[[ability:cloak-of-shadows]] however, which they should be doing, your priest has to wait for the cloaks to expire before trinketing and fearing. Once they trinket one of the [[ability:fear]], you have the option to [[ability:blind]] or [[ability:cheap-shot]] [[ability:kidney-shot]] whoever trinketed, or [[ability:cheap-shot]] [[ability:kidney-shot]] one and [[ability:blind]] the other, and win the game.

Save your trinket for the [[ability:kidney-shot]] in this matchup, and [[ability:cloak-of-shadows]] the wounds off whenever your priest has a chance to heal you.

If they [[ability:sap]] you and open on your priest, the way you play it out is the same. Your priest tries to find a time to trinket and [[ability:fear]] them, and once he does, you react with your trinket and stun/[[ability:blind]] them.

You have the option to pre-[[ability:vanish]] if they [[ability:sap]] your priest and pop your perception as well, but that's assuming you know you're facing a double rogue and not a rogue druid.

Just be quick with your trinket/[[ability:fear]]/blind/vanish stuns and play reactively. Double rogue is just a stupid comp and there isn't much more to say on it.
